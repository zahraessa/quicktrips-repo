Research

Related projects

TravelRepublic[0]

This website consists of a holiday generator which allows you to specify the type of holiday you want to go on through the series of 3 questions then generates a recommendation.

It allows you to see other similar recommendations based on attributes such as “hotter” or “more shopping”.

This then displays more information about the location and allows you to search for hotel and flight packages at the selected location.

Overall it has a very intuitive UI and it features similar features to our application.

However, the recommendations are not based on the users travel date and price preferences and so may not result in an as personalized holiday as QuickTrips does.

Holiday Gems[1] Where should I go on holiday? Quiz:

This quiz gores through a series of questions allowing the user to specify the time of year, temperature, travel distance, activities and who you’re travelling with to generate a holiday.

These are some of the key features that we use to generate our recommendations.

However, it doesn’t let you specify your trip length or budget prior to the recommendation which means that the result may not be specific to the user’s needs.

Furthermore, the user is only presented with a single recommendation based on their inputs which means that flexibility is limited.

Related technologies

Templete and Design

We are using fonts from Google fonts[2], icon[3] and Bootstrap[4] for our front-end design and themes from the following free source:

For major navigate bar, we had looked at Grayscale[5]

For user profile, we had looked at the design in 6.Teammate Profile in Mockplus[6]

For questionnaire page, we had used checkout example from Bootstrap[7] for the one linked with flask and the new design had referenced Form with Multiple Steps example from w3schools.com[8]

For checkbox in keyword page, we had looked at Custom Checkbox example from w3schools.com[9]

For favourite button on the right bottom corner of detail page, we had looked at the social button in Coming Soon[10]

For all square buttons, we had looked at the buttons in Grayscale[5]

For recommendation in home page, we had referenced Creative in Start Bootstrap[11]

For user comment part in homepage, we had looked at Agency example[12]

For login and register page, we had looked at the SB ADmin2 example[13] and button of Google and Facebook had referenced the design in Coming Soon[10] in Start Bootstrap

Front End

Alternatives to Bootstrap[4] that we could have used was Flexbox Grid[14] or PureCSS[15]. However, we found the Bootstrap templates to be more user friendly and professional thus making Bootstrap a more suitable technology to use.

Back End

For the Back-End Development we considered to main frameworks: Python’s Flask[16] Framework and Java’s Spring[17] Framework. Spring was a good alternative as its opensource and we are very familiar with coding in Java. However, the Flask framework is more lightweight and easier to use.
